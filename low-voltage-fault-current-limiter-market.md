<p><p>The low voltage fault current limiter market comprises two main types: superconducting fault current limiters (SFCL) and non-superconducting fault current limiters (NSFCL). SFCLs utilize superconducting materials to effectively manage and reduce fault currents with minimal energy loss, enhancing system reliability. In contrast, NSFCLs employ resistive or inductive elements to limit fault currents, generally at a lower cost and with simpler technology. Both types are essential for protecting electrical grids and equipment from damage during fault conditions.</p></p>
