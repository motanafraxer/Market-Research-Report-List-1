<p><p>The glucose market comprises two primary forms: D-Glucose Monohydrate and Anhydrous Glucose. D-Glucose Monohydrate contains a single water molecule per glucose unit, making it particularly suitable for various food and pharmaceutical applications due to its stability and solubility. In contrast, Anhydrous Glucose is devoid of water, offering higher concentration and longer shelf life, making it ideal for industries requiring efficient storage and transport. Both types are essential for energy supply in food, beverages, and medical formulations.</p></p>
