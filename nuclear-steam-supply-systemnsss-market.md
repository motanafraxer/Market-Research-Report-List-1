<p><p>The Nuclear Steam Supply System (NSSS) is a crucial component of nuclear power plants, responsible for generating steam to drive turbines. There are three primary market types: Pressurized Water Reactors (PWR) maintain high pressure to prevent boiling, using water as both coolant and moderator. Boiling Water Reactors (BWR) allow water to boil directly in the reactor core, producing steam for turbine generation. Pressurized Heavy Water Reactors (PHWR) utilize heavy water as both moderator and coolant, enhancing efficiency and fuel use. Each type serves specific operational and regulatory markets.</p></p>
