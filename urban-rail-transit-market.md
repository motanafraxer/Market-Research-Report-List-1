<p><p>Urban rail transit encompasses various systems designed for efficient city transportation. Metro rail systems, characterized by high capacity and speed, often operate underground or on exclusive tracks. Light rail systems serve shorter distances with lower capacity, integrating into street-level traffic and providing greater flexibility. Monorails use a single rail for support, allowing for elevated structures with minimal land use, ideal for urban settings with space constraints. Each type caters to specific transit needs, enhancing urban mobility.</p></p>
