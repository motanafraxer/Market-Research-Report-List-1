<p><p>Nuclear imaging devices primarily include Positron Emission Tomography (PET) and Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT). PET employs radioactive tracers to visualize metabolic processes, commonly used in oncology, cardiology, and neurology. SPECT, on the other hand, provides detailed images by detecting gamma rays, offering information on blood flow and functional activity; it's widely used in diagnosing heart diseases and brain disorders. Both technologies enhance diagnostic accuracy, guiding treatment decisions and monitoring response to therapies.</p></p>
