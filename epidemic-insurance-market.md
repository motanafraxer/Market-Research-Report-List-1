<p><p>Epidemic insurance can be categorized into two main market types: life insurance and non-life insurance. Life insurance covers the financial impact of increased mortality rates due to epidemics, providing beneficiaries with payouts following policyholders' deaths. Non-life insurance, on the other hand, encompasses policies that address property loss, business interruption, and liability claims stemming from epidemic-related disruptions. Together, these markets aim to mitigate risks associated with public health crises, offering financial protection and support during uncertain times.</p></p>
